Overview

Physically unclonable functions (PUFs) return a unique response to a challenge, expose manufacturing variance intrinsic to IC fabrication, and currently require dedicated hardware and proprietary software.

With PUFLib, our goal is to develop an open-source, modular library to use PUFs for data protection using Intel x86 and ARM, a variety of “legacy” and novel PUF plug-ins, and a modular design to allow others to contribute. We also plan to provide seal()/unseal() semantics for device-specific sealed storage, and demonstrate to LEG team to support a data loss prevention (DLP)-type solution.

Our approach includes both legacy and novel sources.

Legacy sources:

  • SRAM (done on ARES)
  • Flash (not done in public)
  • FPGA (not done under this effort)

Novel sources:

  • DDR RAM (new!)
  • SMBus EEPROM (new!)

The PUFLib core includes automatic provisioning of hardware-supported PUF sources, error-correcting codes for temp/age variance, and Shamir’s secret sharing for multi-PUF support and additional error tolerance.
